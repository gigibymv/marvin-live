I.1 · A diagnosis
DatedApril 8, 2026 ContextAdvisory, $900B market Horizon18 months Source14 partner interviews,
312 engagements reviewed

Every generation of consulting firms has been an answer to a question about leverage. The 1960s asked how a few smart generalists could advise many CEOs at once — the answer was the pyramid. The 1990s asked how the pyramid could industrialize — the answer was the deck. The 2020s are asking something different, and the honest answer is that no one in this building has finished the sentence.

What we know is this: the analyst tier is being dissolved, quietly, by tools our own juniors use off-policy on their phones. What we do not know is what replaces the pyramid — but we have been running the experiment for fourteen months, and the results are in the next twenty pages.

VII · House principles
VII · Principles
of the house

Eight rules we will not trade away.

i.

A human signs every page.

No deliverable leaves the firm without a named partner attesting to it. Agents draft; humans are accountable.

ii.

Uncertainty is a first-class citizen.

We report confidence intervals, not point estimates. When we do not know, we say so.

iii.

Your data stays your data.

Agents run inside isolated enclaves you can audit. Nothing you share trains a model outside your walls.

iv.

We price the answer, not the hour.

Fixed fees per question. When agents make us faster, you pay less.

v.

The deliverable is replayable.

Every engagement ships with a console. You can re-run our analysis on new data, six months later, without us.

vi.

We leave you more capable.

A successful engagement ends with agents operating inside your team, not ours.

vii.

Small rooms, short memos.

Four humans, nine agents, one page. We refuse the theater of the fifty-slide deck.

viii.

The partner takes the call.

No gatekeepers, no assistants, no queues. The partner who sold the work does the work.
